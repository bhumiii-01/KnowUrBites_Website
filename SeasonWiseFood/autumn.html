üåø Healthy Tips

Include warming spices like cinnamon, ginger, and cloves to boost immunity.

Opt for seasonal produce and whole grains to stay energized and balanced.

Include foods rich in Vitamin C (like citrus, kiwi) and zinc(nuts, seeds) to stay strong as the weather cools.

Stay Hydrated as cooler weather can trick you into drinking less water.

Shorter days can affect energy, so keep a steady sleep schedule.

üìç Regional Highlights

Punjab: Sarson da saag and makki di roti are autumn staples.

Maharashtra: Bhakarwadi and sweet potato dishes mark the season.

Uttarakhand: Jhangora kheer and mandua roti are traditional autumn fare.

Uttar Pradesh & Bihar: Guavas and pomegranates in season; sweet potato snacks common.

West Bengal: Khichuri, labra (mixed veg), sweets like sandesh and rasgulla.

üéâ Cultural Connection

Autumn is a time of harvest and celebration, reflected in the abundance of flavors.

Durga Puja (bhog with khichuri, labra), Navratri (fast-friendly dishes), Diwali (sweets & snacks).

Use of kuttu, sabudana, pumpkin, and sweet potato in vrat (fasting) foods links diet with devotion.

Seasonal crops like paddy, millets, and pulses shape community meals and rituals.

Autumn is the peak for making laddoos, halwas, barfis, and other mithai as offerings and festive sharing.
