1. Stones in Pulses

Visual inspection is the easiest method. Spread the pulses on a flat surface and remove visible stones manually.

2. Lead Chromate in Dal

Add a few drops of hydrochloric acid. A pink color indicates the presence of toxic lead chromate.Need lab test.

3. Iron Pieces in Rice

Use a magnet to detect iron filings or fragments mixed with rice grains.

4. Colored Grains in Pulses

Rub the grains between moist palms. If color comes off, synthetic dye has been used to enhance appearance.

5. Mud Balls in Wheat

Soak wheat in water. Mud balls will dissolve or disintegrate, while real grains remain intact.

6. Polished Rice with Glucose or Talc

Rub rice between fingers. If it feels powdery or leaves a white residue, it may be coated with glucose or talc.

7. Kesari Dal in Chana Dal

Kesari dal is banned due to neurotoxicity. Lab testing is recommended for accurate detection.

8. Insect Eggs in Stored Grains

Soak grains in water. Eggs and larvae float to the surface, while clean grains sink.
