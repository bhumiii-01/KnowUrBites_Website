1. Formalin in Frozen Fish

Dip fish in warm water and smell. A pungent chemical odor indicates formalin, used illegally as a preservative.

2. Excess Ice Coating

Check for thick ice layers on frozen items. This may be used to increase weight and hide spoilage.

3. Repeated Freezing

Look for ice crystals inside packaging. Repeated freezing causes texture breakdown and may indicate poor handling.

4. Artificial Coloring in Frozen Vegetables

Soak vegetables in water. If color leaches out, synthetic dyes may have been used to enhance appearance.

5. Spoiled Frozen Meat

Thaw and inspect for sour smell, slimy texture, or discoloration. These are signs of spoilage or chemical treatment.

6. Preservative Overuse in Ready Meals

Check labels for sodium benzoate or sorbic acid. Excessive use may cause allergic reactions or digestive issues.

7. Contaminated Ice Cubes

Cloudy ice or uneven melting may indicate use of unfiltered water. Avoid if hygiene is questionable.

8. Texture Loss in Frozen Paneer

Frozen paneer should remain firm after thawing. Spongy or crumbly texture may indicate poor quality or additives.
