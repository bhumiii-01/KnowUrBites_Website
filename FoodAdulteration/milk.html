1. Water in Milk

Place a drop of milk on a polished surface. If it flows quickly without leaving a white trail, water may be present.

2. Starch in Milk

Add a few drops of iodine solution(available at most pharmacies) . A blue coloration indicates the presence of starch.

3. Detergent in Milk

Shake 5‚Äì10 ml of milk with an equal amount of water. Excess foam formation suggests detergent adulteration.

4. Synthetic Milk

Synthetic milk tastes bitter, feels soapy when rubbed, and turns yellow on heating. Avoid consumption.

5. Urea in Milk

Mix milk with soybean powder and arhar (pigeon pea) powder. A blue color indicates urea presence. Lab confirmation recommended.

6. Formalin in Milk

Add a few drops of sulfuric acid (Cleaning Products) to the milk. A violet ring at the junction indicates formalin, a harmful preservative.

7. Starch in Paneer

Boil a small piece of paneer in water, cool, and add iodine. A blue color confirms starch adulteration.

8. Soap in Ghee

Mix ghee with water and shake. Foam formation suggests soap adulteration.
