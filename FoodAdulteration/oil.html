1. Argemone Oil in Mustard Oil

Rub a few drops of oil on your palms.A chemical smell or an unusual, sharp odor indicates adulteration.

2. Mineral Oil Detection

Shake the oil with equal volume of alcohol. Mineral oil forms a uniform layer, unlike pure oil.

3. Mixed Oils in Coconut Oil

Refrigerate the sample. Pure coconut oil solidifies fully; mixed oils remain partially liquid.

4. Castor Oil in Edible Oils

Mix with petroleum ether. Castor oil separates as a distinct layer due to its density.

5. Rancidity in Oils

Smell the oil. Rancid oils emit a sharp, unpleasant odor and may taste bitter. Heating may cause early smoking.

6. Palmolein in Groundnut Oil

place a sample in the refrigerator for 2-4 hours; if it solidifies, it is likely adulterated with palmolein, as pure groundnut oil should remain liquid

7. Castor Oil in Coconut Oil

the presence of castor oil in coconut oil by observing the thickness . Coconut oil is much lighter and will feel smooth, while castor oil is thick and viscous.

8. Sesame Oil in Other Oils

Add fertilizers and sugar to the oil. A red color indicates sesame oil.

9. Coloring Agents

Rub oil on white paper and expose to sunlight. Artificial colors leave a visible stain.
