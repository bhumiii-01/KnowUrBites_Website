üç≠ Simple tests to detect adulterants in sugar, jaggery, and honey

1. Chalk Powder in Sugar

Dissolve sugar in water. Chalk powder settles at the bottom, while pure sugar dissolves completely.

2. Washing Soda in Sugar

Dissolve the sugar in water and shake well. If the solution froths, it suggests the presence of washing powder.

3. Lead Salts in Sugar

Not safe for home testing. Lead salts are toxic and require lab analysis.

4. Chalk Powder in Jaggery

Mix jaggery in water. If chalk is present, it settles at the bottom as residue.

5. Artificial Sweeteners in Honey

Dip a cotton wick in honey and light it. Pure honey burns cleanly; adulterated honey may crackle or resist burning.

6. Sugar Syrup in Honey

Place a drop of honey on blotting paper. Pure honey stays firm; sugar syrup spreads or gets absorbed.

7. Water in Honey

Dip a matchstick in honey and strike. If it lights easily, honey is pure. Watered honey prevents ignition.
