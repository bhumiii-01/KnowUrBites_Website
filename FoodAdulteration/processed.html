Processed & Packaged Foods

üì¶ Simple checks to detect adulterants in ready-to-eat and packaged items

1. Expired Ingredients

Check manufacturing and expiry dates. Avoid products with faded or tampered labels.

2. Non-Permitted Preservatives

Review ingredient list for banned chemicals like boric acid or formalin. Report suspicious items.

3. Excess Salt or Sugar

Taste and compare with standard versions. Excessive amounts may be used to mask spoilage.

4. Artificial Flavor Enhancers

Check for monosodium glutamate (MSG) or synthetic flavor codes. May cause allergic reactions in sensitive individuals.

5. Packaging Tampering

Inspect seals and wrappers. Broken or resealed packaging may indicate contamination or counterfeiting.

6. Synthetic Coloring

Bright unnatural colors in snacks or sweets may indicate synthetic dyes. Rub with moist cotton to test.

7. Spoiled Ready Meals

Check for sour smell, bloated packaging, or discoloration. These are signs of microbial spoilage.

8. Unlisted Allergens

Always read labels for allergen warnings. Some products may omit key ingredients like nuts or gluten.
