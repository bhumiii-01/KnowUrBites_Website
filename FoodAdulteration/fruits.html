Fruits and Vegetables

1. Wax Coating on Fruits

Scratch the surface of apples or pears. If wax peels off or feels greasy, artificial wax may be present.

2. Malachite Green on Green Vegetables

Soak leafy vegetables in water. If the water turns green, synthetic dye like malachite green has been used.

3. Rhodamine B on Red Chilies

Dip red chilies in water. If the water turns pink, it may contain Rhodamine B, a harmful dye.

4. Artificial Coloring on Tomatoes

Rub the surface with cotton soaked in water. If color comes off, synthetic dye has been applied.

5. Calcium Carbide in Mangoes

Calcium carbide ripened mangoes have a uniform yellow color and emit a garlic-like smell. Avoid such fruits.

6. Lead Salts in Green Peas

Boil peas in water. If the water turns green, it may indicate the presence of lead salts. Lab testing recommended.

7. Excess Pesticide Residue

Wash fruits and vegetables thoroughly. A white film or sticky residue may indicate pesticide contamination.

8. Synthetic Color in Carrots

Rub carrots with a moist cloth. If color transfers, synthetic dye may be present.
