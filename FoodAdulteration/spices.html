1. Lead Chromate in Turmeric

dissolve a small amount of powder in water; pure turmeric will turn the water light yellow, while the presence of lead chromate will cause it to turn a bright, intense yellow or release colorful streaks.

2. Brick Powder in Chili Powder

Sprinkle chili powder on water. Brick powder settles quickly, while pure chili floats and spreads slowly.

3. Colored Sawdust in Turmeric Powder

Mix turmeric in water. Colored sawdust floats and releases color; pure turmeric settles without color spread.

5. Iron Filings in Tea Leaves

Spread tea leaves on paper and move a magnet over them. Iron filings will be attracted to the magnet.

6. Yellow Lead Salts in Mustard Seeds

7. Excess Coloring in Asafoetida

Dissolve in water. Pure asafoetida gives a milky white solution; synthetic coloring gives a bright yellow tint.

8. Chalk Powder in Salt

Dissolve salt in water. Chalk powder settles at the bottom, while pure salt dissolves completely.
