1. Artificial Color in Fruit Juices

Mix juice with water. If color separates or settles, synthetic dye may be present.

2. Excess Sugar in Soft Drinks

Evaporate a small amount of the drink. Excess sugar leaves behind sticky residue or crystals.

3. Metal Contamination in Packaged Drinks

Check for metallic taste or odor. If suspected, avoid consumption and report to authorities.

4. Iron Filings in Tea Leaves

Spread tea leaves on paper and move a magnet over them. Iron filings will be attracted to the magnet.

5. Used Tea Leaves in Packaged Tea

Boil tea and spread the residue. Used leaves appear dull and brittle compared to fresh ones.

6. Synthetic Flavor in Soft Drinks

Artificial flavors often leave a chemical aftertaste. Natural drinks taste fresh and fruity.

7. Caffeine Overdose in Energy Drinks

Check the label for caffeine content. Excessive intake may cause restlessness or insomnia.

8. Contaminated Ice in Street Beverages

Ice made from unfiltered water may appear cloudy and melt unevenly. Avoid if hygiene is doubtful.
