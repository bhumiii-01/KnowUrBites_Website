Meat, Fish & Eggs

1. Rotten Meat

Check for foul odor, slimy texture, and greenish discoloration. Fresh meat should be firm and odorless.

2. Formalin in Fish

Dip a piece of fish in water and smell. Formalin-preserved fish has a pungent chemical odor and stiff texture.

3. Ammonia in Frozen Meat

Thaw and smell the meat. Ammonia-treated meat emits a sharp, irritating odor and may cause eye discomfort.

4. Synthetic Coloring in Meat

Rub the surface with moist cotton. If color transfers, synthetic dye may have been used to enhance appearance.

5. Spoiled Eggs

Place eggs in water. Fresh eggs sink and lie flat; spoiled eggs float due to gas buildup inside the shell.

6. Stale Fish

Check for sunken eyes, dull scales, and soft belly. Fresh fish has bright eyes and firm flesh.

7. Contaminated Chicken

Inspect for sticky surface, sour smell, and excessive water retention. These may indicate spoilage or chemical treatment.

8. Adulterated Minced Meat

Check for uneven texture and color. Adulterated mince may contain fillers like starch or offal.
