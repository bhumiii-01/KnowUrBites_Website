1. Use of Non-Permitted Colors

Rub the surface of colored bakery items with a moist cotton ball. If color transfers, synthetic dye may be present.

2. Excess Baking Soda in Bread

Excess soda gives a bitter taste and may cause bloating. Check ingredient labels and taste carefully.

3. Use of Vanaspati in Cakes

Vanaspati is sometimes used instead of butter. It leaves a greasy texture and lacks natural aroma.

4. Synthetic Flavor in Biscuits

Artificial flavors may leave a chemical aftertaste. Natural flavors are subtle and pleasant.

5. Contaminated Cream in Pastries

Check for sour smell or curdled texture. Spoiled cream may cause stomach discomfort.

6. Use of Saccharin in Low-Calorie Bakes

Saccharin leaves a metallic aftertaste. Check labels for approved sweeteners like stevia or sucralose.

7. Mold Growth in Stored Bread

Inspect for green or black spots. Moldy bread should be discarded immediately.

8. Excess Oil in Fried Bakery Items

Touch the surface. If it feels overly greasy or leaves oil stains, it may contain excess reused oil.
